## Module 1 — Response of the Series RLC Circuit

Source: C:\Users\aknoesen\Documents\Knoesen\ENG17 Experiment\ENG17-series-RLC-learning-objectives.md

Key concepts introduced in Module 1 that carry forward:
- KVL, KCL, Ohm's law assumed fluent
- Series circuit current continuity: all elements in a series loop share the same current
- Continuity of capacitor voltage: \(v_C(0^+) = v_C(0^-)\)
- Continuity of inductor current: \(i_L(0^+) = i_L(0^-)\)
- Power in a resistor: \(p = i^2 R = v^2/R\)
- Second-order differential equations and their characteristic polynomials
- Concept of steady-state vs. transient response decomposition

Students entering Module 2 should be comfortable applying KVL/KCL and
recognizing that circuit variables can or cannot change instantaneously
depending on the element type.
